\section{Hardware and Simulation Verification Captures}
\label{sec:app_verification_captures}



Waveform captures from physical oscilloscope measurements and VHDL simulations illustrate key verification results from Layer 1 testing. While not all measurements were archived as images, the following examples demonstrate the verification approach.



\begin{figure}[htbp]

	\centering

	\includegraphics[width=0.9\textwidth]{watchdog_reaction.png}

	\caption[Oscilloscope Capture: Watchdog Circuit Reaction]{An oscilloscope capture demonstrating the hardware verification approach (Layer 1). This measurement shows the watchdog circuit's behavior, confirming the correct temporal interaction between the FPGA's servicing pulses (yellow) and the watchdog's reset assertion (blue). This method was similarly applied to verify other critical timing parameters, such as the frequency sweep.}
	\label{fig:watchdog_reaction}

\end{figure}



\begin{figure}[htbp]

	\centering

	\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{spi_testbench_error.png}

	\caption[VHDL Testbench for SPI Transmission]{A VHDL testbench waveform demonstrating the simulation-based verification approach (Layer 1). This simulation confirms the correct 32-bit frame transmission, which is truncated to 16 bits on the receiver side due to an STM32 configuration mismatch, as discussed in \Cref{subsec:limitations}. Image adapted from Savchuk et al.~\cite{savchuk2025}.}
	\label{fig:spi_testbench}

\end{figure}
